Application Domain

This project is the requirements and design of an accounting system that's intended to be comparable to Sage 50 and/or QuickBooks. This development is to provide an enterprise grade accounting system while keeping simplicity. The system should provide the following:

This demotes that a customer can have a quote provided that is then turned into and order. When the order has been shipped it's turned into a invoice. If items are bad and have to be returned, the credit is created and any payments sent and received. The same applies to vendors. Finally, all transactions may be related to a "job". A job is any business activity that we need to track.

This does not show the back end accounting of journal entries, account reconciliation, setup, and reporting.

Requirements

This covers what is required for the first public release. This is broken into functional and non-functional requirements. A functional requirement is a business task to be completed, while non-functional requirements are everything else that must be considered.

Functional Requirements

This section covers the business objective the application will be used to complete a task.

  1. Accounting Core
    1. Accounts
      These are the "accounting" accounts of asset, liability, equity, revenue and expense. We must our types should account for year end closing considerations (close to retained earnings). Accounts should allow recursive definitions making a "tree" structure.
    2. Journal Entry
      This is the basis for all documents in the program. Non-posting transactions will enter "0.00" lines. The jounal entry should allow for tagging of lines to contacts, jobs, departments (?).
    3. Register
      I would use the term check register, but that's almost dated. But we should provide a "register" style entry for quick coding of account transactions.
    4. Bank Deposits
    5. Reconciliation
      We should be able to "import" bank statements and create the missing transactions.
    6. Contacts
      Contact are usually separate lists requiring a redefinition is a vendor is also a customer. We should let vendors and customers "inherit" from contacts.
  2. Inventory
    1. Items
      Item list management where items denote the the following purchases, sales, and inventory: description, unit measure, price, account.
      1. Non-Stock
        This is non tracked item definitions that can be used through the documents.
      2. Inventory
        This is just a cycle count of bought and sold. The quantities on documents denote a unit bought or sold. This should include the ability to tag with locations.
      3. Serialization
        For each quantity bought or sold, we must document the serial number.
    2. Unit Measures
    3. Unit Conversions
      Math can be used to convert measure of like types. if we have an equivalency: 
      16 oz = 1 lbs
1 in = 2.24 mm
1 barrel = 55 gal
    4. Assemblies
      Assemblies are definitions of formulas or recipes to build a product or service. Once "built" a new item will exist. It needs to be defined that a bunch of items go in, and one item comes out.
    5. Builds
      Normal accounting systems account for build as definitive "recipes" for making a product. When a build is completed, we should account for the "actual" quantities used.
    6. Valuations
    7. Item Status
    8. Item History
  3. Receivables
    1. Customer
    2. Tax Groups
    3. Tax Authorities
    4. Sales Quote
    5. Sales Order
    6. Sales Invoice
    7. Sales Credit
    8. Receives Payment
    9. Aged Receivables
    10. Customer History
    11. Taxable Sales
  4. Payables
    1. Vendors
    2. Vendor Quote
    3. Purchase Order
    4. Vendor Invoice
    5. Vendor Credit
    6. Send Payment
    7. Aged Payables
    8. Vendor History
  5. Human Resources
    1. Employees
    2. Time Entry
    3. Paycheck
